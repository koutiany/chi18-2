The journey of a Chinese Buddhist monk in the 7th century

The Story

He left his home and started a journey to India, searching for the complete Buddhist texts. 19 years, a hundred and ten countries, he left when he was a novice monk and came back as a master. Under his effort, 47 Buddhist Scriptures, in total 1335 volumes were translated, the reputation of the Tang Dynasty was broadly casted, and the communication between Chinese and Indian Buddhists moved to the next level. His story inspired many literary works, including Journey to the West (Xi You Ji, wikipedia link), later to be considered as one of the Four Great Classical Novels of Chinese literature. He is Xuanzang.

The Great Tang Records on the Western Regions is a narrative written by his disciple. The goal of this project is to present the first part of his journey, from China to India, in an clear way. Remember, the journey happened a thousand and three hundred years ago, when transportation heavily relied on horses or simply walking.
